DEAD POETS SOCIETY
20.06.2024
“We don’t read and write poetry because it’s cute. We read and write poetry because we are members of the human race. And the human race is filled with passion. Medicine, law, business, engineering, these are noble pursuits and necessary to sustain life. But poetry, beauty, romance, love, these are what we stay alive for,” says John Keating in “Dead Poets Society.”

This novel, based on the screenplay by N.H. Kleinbaum, is one of the best teen drama books. “Dead Poets Society” follows Todd Anderson and his friends as they revive the Dead Poets Society, a secret club once led by their poetry teacher, Mr. Keating. The story chronicles their adventures within the club and their inspiring interactions with Mr. Keating. One of the standout aspects of the book is Mr. Keating himself.

Mr. Keating’s unorthodox teaching methods and passionate demeanor make readers feel as if they are part of the story. He inspires not only his students but also everyone who reads the book. Mr. Keating encourages each of his students to pursue what they love. Neil Perry is one such student, but his dream of becoming an actor clashes with his family’s expectations. Neil's parents eventually pull him out of school and send him to military school.

The book is filled with thrilling moments and unexpected events, offering readers a new perspective on life. It can be compared to “The Secret History” by Donna Tartt and “The Song of Achilles” by Madeline Miller, as all these books explore the lives of teenagers striving for extraordinary experiences.

The “Dead Poets Society” movie is rated PG-13 and is intended for teens aged 15-18. This book is an amazing read, and I would give it a 5/5. For anyone looking for a new book to read, “Dead Poets Society” is an excellent choice.
